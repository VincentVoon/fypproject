What is Home Nursing Care?

Home nursing care is home-based care provided to patients who require professional support for their acute or chronic conditions. Receiving nursing care at home enables you to focus on recovery and rehabilitation in a comfortable and familiar space.

Moreover, receiving private care in hospitals, clinics or medical facilities can become costly in the long run and home nursing care provides you with a more affordable and practical option

The need for accessible on-demand home nursing care in Malaysia

After surgery or diagnosis, our focus should be on self-care and resting well for a full recovery. However, the reality is that we tend to spend much of our time and energy working out viable care arrangements, from transportation and queues to cost management, to get the care we need after discharge.

For those with chronic conditions, visits to hospitals, clinics or treatment facilities to get nursing procedures completed can be a common and regular occurrence. However, medical appointments are often time-consuming and the commute can be costly and a hassle, especially for those with mobility constraints.

Sometimes, we may also suddenly find ourselves in need of urgent nursing care due to unforeseen circumstances such as the displacement of a nasogastric tube (NG tube). Speed is key in such scenarios. Travelling to a care facility and waiting in line may not be possible.

What can home nursing help with?

Simple Nursing Care

Assist with simple nursing procedures such as changing of feeding tubes and suctioning.

Complex Nursing Procedures

From injections to wound care, have your nursing procedures done safely in the comfort of home.

Medication Administration

No more missed doses. Our nurses can help dispense and administer the right dosage of medication at the right time.

Receive quality nursing care in Malaysia, hassle-free

Besides hospitals and care facilities, nursing procedures can also be completed at home. Qualified home nurses can bring personalised nursing care you need to your doorstep, saving the hassle and cost of travelling, and also skipping long queues. Most importantly, you can rest and recover in the comfort of your home immediately after the procedure.

Home nurses are also able to assess potential safety risks at home that may hinder your recovery process. For example, they may recommend simple home modifications such as placing a rug on a slippery floor or installing grab bars in the toilets of seniors with high fall risk.

With a specialised understanding of medication and drugs, home nurses can also support with medication management, and ensure that the right medications are being taken at the right times to control health conditions and prevent harmful drug interactions.
